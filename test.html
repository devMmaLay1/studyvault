Section 1

The Active Voice

The active voice is the most direct and natural way to write a sentence. The subject does the action — it acts on the object.

Subject (S)  →  Verb (V)  →  Object (O)

Simple Definition

  • The subject performs the action directly.
  • There is no swapping of subject and object.
  • It is the clearest, most direct way to express an idea.

Real-Life Examples

Example 1
John ate the yam.
John (S) → ate (V) → the yam (O)
Example 2
She grabbed his hand.
She (S) → grabbed (V) → his hand (O)
Example 3
The teacher marked the scripts.
Teacher (S) → marked (V) → scripts (O)
Example 4
NEPA took the light at midnight.
NEPA (S) → took (V) → the light (O)
⚠️ Watch out — Complement Objects!
Some sentences look active but are NOT. If the object says something further about the verb (a complement), it cannot be reversed into passive.

❌ "The boy is in the garden." — no real object to swap.
❌ "She is a beautiful girl." — "a beautiful girl" is a complement, not an object.
Quick Exercise — Identify the S, V, O
1. Everybody needs a friend.
2. The dog bit the man.
3. My mother cooked jollof rice.
✅ Answers
1. Everybody (S) → needs (V) → a friend (O)
2. The dog (S) → bit (V) → the man (O)
3. My mother (S) → cooked (V) → jollof rice (O)
Section 2

The Passive Voice

In the passive voice, the object of the active sentence becomes the subject. The focus shifts from who did it to what was done.

Object becomes Subject  +  be (correct tense)  +  Past Participle  +  by [doer]

How to Convert Active → Passive

Active VoicePassive Voice
He cleared his throat. His throat was cleared by him.
Everybody needs a girl. A girl is needed by everybody.
She grabbed his hand. His hand was grabbed by her.
He noticed the girl. The girl was noticed by him.
Key changes when converting:
• she → her  |  he → him  |  they → them
• Add by before the doer: "by him", "by her", "by Silas"
• Change the verb: needs → is needed  |  noticed → was noticed

Passive Forms Across All Tenses

TenseFormulaExample
Simple Present / Past
am/is/are/was/were + past participle		 "Energy is used every day."
"The letter was written."
Present Continuous
is/are + being + past participle		 "Changes are being made."

has/have + been + past participle		 "This topic has been discussed."
Future
will + be + past participle		 "The answer will be known."
Modal Verbs
can/must + be + past participle		 "Food production can be increased."

When to Use the Passive Voice

  • When the doer is unknown — "He was killed in a fight." (We don't know who killed him.)
  • When the doer is not important — focus is on what happened, not who did it.
  • In scientific writing — "Glass is made from sand which is melted under great heat."
  • In historical events — "The First Battle of Panipat was fought in 1526."
  • When you want to be impersonal — "He saw John" (personal) → "John was seen" (impersonal).

Real-Life Examples

Science
Glass is made from sand.
Doer (factory workers) not important.
News / History
The president was assassinated.
Focus on the event, not the killer.
Official Notice
You have been informed about the case.
Impersonal, formal tone.
Everyday
The meeting was addressed by the Governor.
Doer mentioned with "by".
⚠️ Common Nigerian Errors with Passive Voice

Always add -ed (past participle) after helping verbs!

❌ "You have been inform about the case."  →  ✅ "You have been informed."
❌ "Your case will be consider tomorrow."  →  ✅ "Your case will be considered."
❌ "He felt unconcern about the issue."  →  ✅ "He felt unconcerned."
❌ "She got me confuse."  →  ✅ "She got me confused."
Quick Exercise — Convert to Passive
1. The police arrested the thief.
2. My mother cooks rice every day.
3. The students will write the exam tomorrow.
✅ Answers
1. The thief was arrested by the police.
2. Rice is cooked by my mother every day.
3. The exam will be written by the students tomorrow.
Section 3

The Infinitive (to + verb)

The infinitive is the base form of a verb, usually with to before it. It is used after other verbs and to express purpose.

to + base verb    (e.g. to go, to eat, to study, to pass)

Use 1 — After Certain Verbs

Some verbs are always followed by to + infinitive:

  • want, wish, hope, decide → "I want to pass my exams."
  • promise, plan, fail, refuse → "She refused to answer."
  • advise, ask, tell + someone → "The doctor advised him to stop smoking."

Use 2 — To Express Purpose (Why someone does something)

Use to + infinitive to explain the reason or purpose of an action:

Purpose Example 1
Azhar went to the bank to cash a cheque.
Why did he go? → to cash a cheque.
Purpose Example 2
She went to the chemist to get medicine.
Why? → to get medicine.
Purpose Example 3
We need a match to light the stove.
Why do we need it? → to light the stove.
Purpose Example 4
I am going to Paris to learn French.
Why go to Paris? → to learn French.

Use 3 — "Too … to" Pattern

This pattern means "so [adjective] that he/she cannot…"

Formula: too + adjective + to + infinitive

"It is very cold. We can't go out." → "It is too cold to go out."
"He is very tired. He can't work." → "He is too tired to work."
"He is very young. He won't understand." → "He is too young to understand."

Use 4 — After "make" and "let" (No "to"!)

  • After make and let, drop the "to": "She let me go." (NOT "to go")
  • "Why did you make us wait?" (NOT "to wait")
  • "My mother wouldn't let me touch it." (NOT "to touch")
⚠️ The Biggest Nigerian Error with Infinitives

The infinitive (to) NEVER takes a past tense or past participle form!

❌ "I did not see him to told him."  →  ✅ "I did not see him to tell him."
❌ "She went there to bought food."  →  ✅ "She went there to buy food."

Rule: Always use the BASE FORM of the verb after "to".
Quick Exercise — Fill in with "to" or leave blank
1. Do you know how ___ repair a watch?
2. My mother wouldn't let me ___ touch it.
3. Would you like ___ join the army?
4. Why did you make us ___ wait so long?
✅ Answers
1. to repair (after "how")
2. [no "to"] — after "let", drop "to"
3. to join (after "would like")
4. [no "to"] — after "make", drop "to"
Quick Exercise — Rewrite using "too … to"
1. He was quite angry. He couldn't speak.
2. It was very dark. I didn't see the signpost.
3. He is very busy. He won't see you now.
✅ Answers
1. He was too angry to speak.
2. It was too dark to see the signpost clearly.
3. He is too busy to see you now.
